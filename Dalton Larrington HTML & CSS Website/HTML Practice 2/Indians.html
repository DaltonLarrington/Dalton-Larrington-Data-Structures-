THE CLEVELAND INDIANS

  • The Cleveland Indians are an American professional baseball team based in Cleveland, Ohio.
  • The Indians compete in Major League Baseball (MLB) as a member club of the American League (AL) Central division.
  • Since 1994, they have played at Progressive Field. The team's spring training facility is at Goodyear Ballpark in Goodyear, Arizona.
  • Since their establishment as a major league franchise in 1901, the Indians have won two World Series championships: in 1920 and 1948, along with nine Central Division titles and six American League pennants.
  • The Indians' current World Series championship drought is the longest active drought.
